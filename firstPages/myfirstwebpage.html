movie details:

Starring: Adeel Akhtar, Burn Gorman, David Bamber, Fiona Shaw, Frances De La Tour, Hattie Morahan, Helena Bonham Carter, Henry Cavill, Louis Partridge, Millie Bobby Brown, Sam Claflin, Susan Wokoma.

Summary: England, 1884 – a world on the brink of change. On the morning of her 16th birthday, Enola Holmes (Millie Bobby Brown) wakes to find that her mother (Helena Bonham Carter) has disappeared, leaving behind an odd assortment of gifts but no apparent clue as to where she’s gone or why. After a free-spirited childhood, Enola suddenly finds herself under the care of her brothers Sherlock (Henry Cavill) and Mycroft (Sam Claflin), both set on sending her away to a finishing school for “proper” young ladies. Refusing to follow their wishes, Enola escapes to search for her mother in London. But when her journey finds her entangled in a mystery surrounding a young runaway Lord (Louis Partridge), Enola becomes a super-sleuth in her own right, outwitting her famous brother as she unravels a conspiracy that threatens to set back the course of history. Director: Harry Bradbeer Genre(s): Adventure, Drama, Mystery, Crime Rating: PG-13 Runtime: 123 min

My thoughts:

I really enjoyed the film, although the sophistication did not reach the level of Arthur Conan Doyle but the film was still very interesting and funny and it gave Sherlock Holmes a feminist twist. The actors were excellent and the ending was very surprising. I highly recommend it. I would give the movie a 8 out of ten.
